Syllabus

Stat 223, Winter 2020

James Blair

Course Description

Advanced R programming skills, advanced data techniques, simulation studies.

Prerequisites: STAT 123.

Learning Outcomes

  • Install R, R packages, and RStudio.
  • Advanced data management, manipulate, and transform data.
  • Solve complex problems using advanced R programming methodology.
  • Perform simulation studies, e.g. Central Limit Theorem, confidence intervals, etc.
  • Gain confidence in R programming.

Computing Platform

The computing platform for STAT 223 is RStudio Cloud. This is a free, browser based RStudio instance. You will need to sign up for your own individual account. All course notes and assignments will be made available on RStudio.cloud.

Homework

Homework will be distributed throught RStudio Cloud and is due as indicated in the assignment and on the assignments page. Homework may be submitted late at a 2 points per 24 hours penalty. Depending on the nature of the homework, it may be graded on correctness or effort. If homework is graded on effort, the following guide will be used:

  • 0: Nothing submitted
  • 6: Little effort is evident
  • 10: Substantial effort is evident but it is not obvious that it is correct
  • 12: Substantial effort is evident and it appears to be correct

After your homework has been graded, a feedback.txt file will be added to your RStudio Cloud project to provide specific feedback on your grade.

Please note that solutions for homework assignments are not terribly difficult to find online. While you are free to use any resources available to complete your homework, homework submissions that are copies of existing solutions will not receive any credit. If your homework appears suspiciously similar to existing solutions, you will not receive any credit unless you can prove originality. Simply changing variable names does not make it your own. You are responsible for submitting homework that reflects your efforts and understanding of the material.

Exams

Both the midterm and the final exam will be take home exams. Exams are designed to assess a student’s mastery of concepts, skills, and connections between lecture and homework. You are allowed to use course notes and resources from the internet to assist you during the exams. However, you are not allowed to discuss the exam with other students or work on the exam together. Exams must be individually completed and submitted.
